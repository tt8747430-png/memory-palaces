# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Mindscape is for anyone who needs to memorize and retain their own material: students and test-takers, language learners, professionals prepping for certifications, and self-directed lifelong learners. There is no single narrow persona — the common thread is a person who wants durable recall and will come back day after day if the app makes studying rewarding rather than a chore.

Their context is almost always a phone, in short bursts — a commute, a break between classes, a few minutes before bed — and often with no reliable network. The job to be done is simple to state and hard to sustain: turn a pile of notes or vocabulary into knowledge that sticks, and keep the habit alive long enough for spaced repetition to do its work.

## Product Purpose

Mindscape turns any material into durable memory through spaced repetition, wrapped in a game that keeps learners returning. Learners organize their knowledge as Folders, Decks, and nested Subdecks of Cards and Questions, then study through several Practice modes — Flashcards, Quiz, Match, and SRS Review. Progress is tracked and rewarded through XP, Levels, Streaks, Achievements, and Badges.

It exists because the tools that got the retention science right (Anki) are unpleasant to use, and the tools that are pleasant to use are shallow. Success is a learner who forms a daily study habit and remembers more — visible in streaks maintained, training days accumulated, and Cards reaching maturity.

## Brand Personality

Calm and premium on the surface, quietly game-like underneath. The craft is the identity: considered spacing, soft daylight-blue glass surfaces, and one well-tuned typeface (Lexend) make Mindscape feel like a tool built with Linear- or Things-grade care, not a toy. The gamification is the engine, not the costume — Levels, Streaks, Achievements, and Badges reward real study and pull the learner back tomorrow, but they arrive as satisfying moments (a celebration, a filling ring, a streak kept) rather than constant noise.

The voice is encouraging without being cutesy and confident without being clinical. Motivating like Duolingo, encouraging and progress-legible like Apple Fitness, and as calm and content-first as Notion or Bear when the learner is actually reading, writing, or reviewing.

## Positioning

The all-in-one study app that makes spaced repetition genuinely habit-forming: Anki's retention science and Quizlet's breadth of study modes, delivered with Duolingo's daily-return gamification and the polish of a premium consumer app — in one coherent, offline-first place instead of a spreadsheet, a quiz tool, and a habit tracker bolted together.

## Anti-references

The enemy is clutter. The interface must stay simple and uncluttered — never a wall of controls, never spreadsheet density, never so many options competing on one screen that the learner has to hunt for the one thing they came to do. Depth belongs behind progressive disclosure, not spread across the surface. When a screen feels busy, the fix is to remove, not to rearrange.

## Design Principles

Craft is the credibility. Every screen should feel considered enough that someone fluent in Linear or Things would trust it; polish is not decoration here, it is the thing that separates Mindscape from the study tools it competes with.

The game serves retention, never the reverse. Gamification earns its place only by bringing the learner back and rewarding genuine study. Rewards are moments, not wallpaper — if a game element is not driving the habit, it is clutter.

Simple beats complete on any single screen. Give each screen one primary job and push everything else into progressive disclosure. An uncluttered surface is a feature the learner feels, not a constraint on what the app can do.

Offline-first, always. The app works fully with no network and treats on-device data as the source of truth. The learner should never wait on a server to study.

One coherent vocabulary. The same component means the same thing and the same action looks the same from screen to screen, in code and in copy (see the ubiquitous language and the shared design system). Consistency is what lets the tool disappear into the task.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Commit to WCAG AA contrast — body text at least 4.5:1, large text at least 3:1 — and hold that bar in both the light daylight theme and the dark theme, treating dark mode as a first-class palette rather than an inverted afterthought. Typography stays legible across the app's fixed type scale and should respect the OS text-size setting. Motion already honors `prefers-reduced-motion` globally at the token layer (animations collapse to a near-instant fallback), and that guarantee must survive future work.
